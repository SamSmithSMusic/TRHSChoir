Vocal Ensembles

Chamber Choir

The Thunder Ridge Chamber Choir is an advanced mixed choir for SATB voices in grades 10-12 and meets during the zero hour of the school day. With an emphasis on community engagement, students in this choir will further develop their individual musicianship and participate in community outreach as they perform engaging and advanced repertoire. Students will experience music of many styles, languages, genres, and difficulty levels while singing in Chamber Choir. They will be expected to fully participate and perform in concerts, tours, and other music-making experiences outlined in the calendar. They will regularly complete voice tests and engage in conversation about their musicianship.

Vivace

The Thunder Ridge Vivace Choir is an advanced touring mixed choir for SATB voices in grades 10-12 and meets during the third hour of the school day. Students in this choir will continue to develop their musical and leadership skills as they learn and perform advanced repertoire. These skills include sight-reading, rhythmic analysis, proper vocal technique, and critical reflection. Students will experience music of many styles, languages, genres, and difficulty levels while singing in Vivace. They will be expected to fully participate and perform in concerts, tours, and other music-making experiences outlined in the calendar. In class, they will complete voice tests, language assessments, and consistently engage in conversation about their musicianship.

Cantemus

Cantemus is an introductory choir for tenor and bass voices in grades 9-12 and meets during the fifth hour of the first trimester. Members of this choir will join Cantabile to form Una Voce during the second and third trimesters. Students in Cantemus will be introduced to music as they learn basic skills that will enable them to become lifelong musicians. These skills include sight-reading, rhythmic analysis, proper vocal technique, and critical reflection. Students will experience music of many styles, languages, and difficulty levels while singing in Cantemus. They will be expected to fully participate and perform in concerts and other music-making experiences outlined in the calendar. Students will be evaluated on overall participation, sight-reading, and critical reflection.

About the Conductor

Rand Matheson currently serves as the Director of Choirs at Thunder Ridge High School, where he directs five curricular ensembles and assists with the school musical.

Rand is a recent graduate of St. Olaf College in Northfield, Minnesota, where he earned a Bachelor of Music in Vocal Music Education. During his time at St. Olaf, Rand was actively involved in the Music Department and served as President of the renowned St. Olaf Choir. He also led the St. Olaf Student Chapter of the American Choral Directors Association (ACDA) as President. In addition to teaching and conducting, Rand is a composer and arranger.
